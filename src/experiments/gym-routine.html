5-Day PPL Routine

A cleaner in-app version of the home gym plan: compact enough to scan before a session, detailed enough to keep progression, exercise intent, and recovery rules visible at a glance.

Goal
Hypertrophy with consistent beginner-friendly progression.
Equipment
Cable machine, barbell, dumbbells, bench.
Intensity
Heavy on days 1-3, controlled volume on days 4-5.
Rest
2-3 min for compounds, 60-90 sec for isolation work.
Frequency 5 sessions / week
Split Push / Pull / Legs / Upper / Lower
Skill Focus Form first, load second
Progression Add 1 rep or a small load weekly
Tracking Log every working set

Training Notes

Heavy days should feel demanding but stable. Volume days should feel repeatable. If a later session is wrecking the next one, back off before the plan turns into random fatigue.

Session Rules

  • Progressive overload Add a rep or a small amount of load each week when form stays honest.
  • 1-2 RIR on working sets Finish with one clean rep still in reserve rather than forcing failure every time.
  • Warm up every pattern Two lighter sets before working weight, especially for the first compound of the session.
  • Volume days stay lighter Restraint is part of the split. Easy enough to recover from is the point.

Weekly Rhythm

Day 1Open the week with your most stable pressing work.
Day 2Prioritize rows and pulldowns with crisp back mechanics.
Day 3Squat and hinge with enough rest to keep reps solid.
Day 4Use volume to practice skill, not to chase fatigue.
Day 5Finish with lighter lower-body and core work that leaves you fresh.
